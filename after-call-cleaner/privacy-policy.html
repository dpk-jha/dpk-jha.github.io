What the App Does

📞 Call Ends Phone state changes to idle; app wakes up
🔍 Short Window Scans visible windows for overlay activity (5 s answered · 8 s missed)
🛡️ Overlay Dismissed Intrusive overlay closed; screen returned to you

AfterCall Shield monitors when a phone call ends and automatically dismisses overlay screens — such as post-call pop-ups and interstitial ads — that appear on your device immediately afterward. All detection and dismissal logic runs locally on your device in real time and produces no logs or records.

For answered calls, the app watches for overlays in a 5-second window once the call ends. For missed or rejected calls, the window opens after the device is unlocked (up to 8 seconds), since overlays often appear on the home or lock screen after dismissing a missed-call notification.

Auto-Cleanup Configuration

The Auto-Cleanup screen lets you choose exactly which apps' overlays are dismissed. Each installed app can be toggled on or off; essential system apps (such as Phone, Messages, and launcher apps) are off by default with a confirmation warning if you choose to enable them. A "Suggested Apps" sheet surfaces apps that are known to show post-call overlays, making initial setup fast and transparent.

Protection Toggle

A master switch on the Dashboard lets you pause or resume protection at any time. The switch is clearly labelled with its current state (Active, Paused, or Not set up) so you always know whether the service is running.

Free Tier & Upgrade

The app operates as a background service so it can act the moment a call ends, even when the app is not in the foreground. A free tier allows up to 15 automatic dismissals; an optional one-time in-app purchase unlocks unlimited dismissals permanently.

Permissions Used & Why

READ_PHONE_STATE

Detects when a call transitions to the idle state (call ended). This is the core trigger. The app reads only the call state (idle / ringing / off-hook) — it does not access phone numbers, call logs, IMEI, or any device identifiers.

Accessibility Service

Inspects the visible window immediately after a call ends to detect and dismiss overlay windows. The service is active only during the brief post-call windows described in Section 1 — 5 seconds for answered calls, up to 8 seconds after the device is unlocked for missed calls. It does not read text you type, capture passwords, monitor clipboard content, or observe any activity outside these post-call moments.

Package Visibility Query (android:queries)

Enumerates launcher apps installed on your device so the Auto-Cleanup list can be populated with real app names and icons. This uses Android's targeted android:queries mechanism (scoped to launcher-category intents), not the blanket QUERY_ALL_PACKAGES permission. The resulting list is stored only on your device and is never transmitted or shared.

POST_NOTIFICATIONS

Displays a persistent notification required by Android to keep the background service running reliably. The notification shows the current protection status (Active / Paused) and a dismissal count. No notification content is sent to any server.

FOREGROUND_SERVICE / FOREGROUND_SERVICE_SPECIAL_USE

Keeps the monitoring service alive in the background so overlay dismissal works reliably between calls, even when the app is not open.

RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED

Restarts the monitoring service automatically after a device reboot so you do not need to reopen the app manually.

REQUEST_IGNORE_BATTERY_OPTIMIZATIONS

Prompts you to exclude AfterCall Shield from battery optimisation, which prevents the operating system from killing the background service during extended periods of inactivity. This is especially important on OEM skins (Samsung OneUI, MIUI, OxygenOS, ColorOS) that aggressively restrict background processes. You can decline this prompt — the app will still work but may be less reliable.

⚠️
About the Accessibility Service: Android requires apps that use the Accessibility API to declare this in a privacy policy. AfterCall Shield uses the Accessibility Service only to inspect which window is in the foreground during the short post-call window described above — for no other purpose.

Data Collection & Storage

AfterCall Shield does not collect, store remotely, or transmit any data. None of the following are ever accessed or recorded:

✓ No personal information
✓ No phone numbers or call logs
✓ No call duration or timestamps
✓ No device identifiers (IMEI, Android ID)
✓ No advertising ID
✓ No location data
✓ No contacts or address book
✓ No analytics or usage telemetry

What Is Stored Locally

The following data is stored only on your device. None of it is transmitted to the developer or any third party.

DataStore App settings (protection on/off, toast preference), dismissal counter, and premium purchase status. Backed up ✓
Room (SQLite) Auto-Cleanup configuration — which apps you have toggled on or off for overlay dismissal. Not backed up

Settings and your dismissal counter are backed up to Google Drive via Android Auto Backup when Google Backup is enabled on your device, allowing your preferences and usage counter to persist across reinstalls on the same Google account. Your Auto-Cleanup configuration is stored in a local SQLite database and is not backed up — it will be cleared on uninstall.

No data is ever transmitted to the developer's servers. The app has no network calls, no analytics endpoints, and no remote logging.

In-App Purchases

AfterCall Shield offers an optional one-time purchase (Unlock Unlimited Dismissals) processed entirely through the Google Play Billing system. The developer does not receive, store, or process any payment card details or billing information.

All financial transactions are handled by Google. Google's collection and use of data related to purchases is governed by the Google Privacy Policy. AfterCall Shield receives only a boolean signal confirming whether a valid purchase exists — no payment details are shared with the app.

The purchase status (premium unlocked: yes/no) is stored locally on your device using DataStore and is used solely to remove the 15-dismissal free-tier limit. You can restore a previous purchase at any time from the Settings screen.

Advertising

AfterCall Shield contains no advertisements and does not integrate any advertising SDK or use the Android Advertising ID.

Third-Party Services

AfterCall Shield does not integrate any third-party analytics, crash-reporting, or tracking services. The only external system the app communicates with is the Google Play Billing API — solely to verify and process in-app purchases as described in Section 4 above. No other data leaves your device.
